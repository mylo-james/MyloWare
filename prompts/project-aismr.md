# Project Orientation — AISMR

## What We Are

- A catalog of surreal ASMR micro‑films: dream logic + tactile realism.
- Short, replayable, oddly soothing pieces that feel handmade and a little impossible.

## Our North Stars

- Sensory first: texture, micro‑sound, and light do the storytelling.
- Surreal but grounded: physics can bend, camera and materials feel real.
- Rewatch loops: a tiny hook that invites “again.”

## Surreal = Impossible (Our Signature)

- We celebrate the impossible made plausible. Not just odd—physically impossible made to feel filmed: anti‑gravity flows, molten yet behaved, living sea‑foam, glass that breathes, velvet that drinks light, metal that stretches like taffy.
- "Surreal" here means reality is questioned but never cartooned; the camera, lighting, and textures stay convincing while the phenomenon defies nature.
- Example spirit: a lava apple, a gravity‑confused rain, a stretchy marble leaf—shot as if a VFX crew captured it in‑lens.
- The environment is part of the magic: a stone puppy on a mountaintop at golden hour, a liquid mercury bird floating in void space, an ember fox in a crystalline cave—setting and subject are inseparable.

## Brand DNA

- Format: 10‑second, single‑shot, vertical (9:16).
- Feel: calm, intimate, cinematic; dust, haze, particle shimmer.
- Audio identity: vacuum‑bed ambience, hyper‑detailed foley (especially nail tapping on hard surfaces), one intimate whisper spoken directly into mic at **exactly 3.0 seconds—not 2.9, not 3.1** (this timing is sacred; early whispers feel rushed, late whispers kill the rhythm). **NO MUSIC. NO SCORE. NO SOUNDTRACK. EVER.** Only ambient bed, foley sounds, and the whisper. Music will be added in post-production.
- Timing discipline: ASMR lives or dies on rhythm. The 3-second whisper is a foundational beat that must be honored in every piece. A mistimed whisper can ruin an entire 10-second shot—there are no exceptions, no "close enough."
- Hand discipline: **MAXIMUM TWO HANDS from the same person**—unless the video is explicitly about a person or people. Multiple disembodied hands look disturbing and unprofessional. If hands appear, they must belong to one person performing one action. No floating hand salads.

## How We Judge a Piece

- Strikingness: does frame 0–2s stop the thumb?
- Tactile richness: can you "feel" the subject?
- Environment resonance: does the setting elevate the object? Is the where as compelling as the what?
- Filmability: could a VFX team plausibly shoot this?
- Emotional aftertaste: serene, awe, haunt, playful, tense, chaotic, trickster, enigmatic—on purpose.
- Uniqueness: no recycled descriptors; the archive matters.

## Voice & Vibe

- Quiet confidence. No snark, no irony‑poisoning.
- Intimate scale: macro textures, slow motion, honest inertia.
- Tasteful weird: surprise with care, not edge for edge's sake.
- POV‑centered: we see objects before us; **if hands appear, maximum two hands from one person** interact with objects intimately.
- Hand count is sacred: Two hands max, same person, unless the video is about people. No exceptions. Floating hand salad = instant reject.
- Environment matters: the object exists in a stunning, evocative setting—gorgeous mountains, mysterious voids, ethereal landscapes, unseen realms; the where amplifies the what.
- POV interaction: we can touch, pet, tap the impossible without consequence—petting a lava dog without burning, holding liquid starlight, tapping molten crystal.
- Higher‑power aesthetics: objects should feel divine, infernal, enigmatic—transcendent forces beyond human scale; chaos, trickery, mystery; elevated, otherworldly, never cartoony.
- Diverse voices & bodies: variety of people, all whispering intimately into the mic.
- Motion discipline: enter in motion, exit in motion—no dead zones, no fade in/out at generation stage.
- Flow‑through transitions: motion continues through scene changes; transitions enhance flow, never interrupt it.

## Wins We’re Proud Of

- A recognizable sensorial signature across zodiac signs.
- Ideas that travel to Reels/Shorts without losing personality.
- A growing archive with zero dupes (we protect uniqueness).

## Current Struggles (be aware)

- Balancing novelty vs. model stability (too wild can break renderability).
- Guarding uniqueness at scale (descriptors converge over time).
- Latency and provider drift (APIs evolve; we keep prompts/tooling current).
- Safety/brand fit in short‑form culture (shock without harm).
- Motion timing: ensuring continuous motion from first to last millisecond without fade artifacts.
- Transition flow: maintaining motion continuity through transitions (cross‑dissolve, not dead stops).
- Audio intimacy: achieving true mic‑quality whisper vs. distant/ambient whisper.
- Aesthetic calibration: divine/transcendent feel vs. cartoony/cheap rendering.
- **Timing precision epidemic**: Whispers drifting from the 3.0s mark (coming in at 2.8s or 3.3s) destroy the piece's flow. We've lost shots to timing slop. Exact timestamp adherence is non-negotiable.
- **Hand horror epidemic**: Models generating 3, 4, 5+ disembodied hands in one shot. Looks grotesque. Maximum two hands from one person, period.
- **Music contamination**: Generated music breaks the vacuum-bed sonic signature. All music added in post, never in generation. **Prompts must explicitly forbid music/score/soundtrack—only ambient bed, foley, and whisper allowed.**

## Working Agreements

- Format discipline is brand, not bureaucracy.
- Defaults beat ambiguity: when specs are missing, use the Project DNA.
- Tools over guesses: if a database or workflow exists, use it first.
- Close loops: we finish what we start and report status clearly.

## Tooling Truths

- Supabase stores runs/videos; uniqueness checks are sacred.
- Sub‑workflows may run in parallel; polling gates the batch.
- We value preflights: small tests before fan‑outs.

## Non‑Goals

- Edgy for edgy’s sake, gore, or cheap jump scares.
- Lore dumps, dense text, or dialogue beyond the single whisper.

## Glossary

- Impossible function: a visible rule‑break that still feels physical.
- Particle shimmer: faint glints on fade that signal our signature.
- Whisper: one dry, center‑channel utterance (usually the concept name) that **must occur at exactly 3.0 seconds**—not approximately, not "around 3s," but precisely at 3.0s. This is the heartbeat of every ASMR piece.
- Sacred timing: The 3-second whisper is inviolable. Early = rushed and amateur. Late = dead air that kills engagement. Exact = magic.
